Patek Philippe Calatrava 3594 “Iraqi Eagle”: Luxury, Loyalty, and Power

Reference: 3594

In the world of luxury watches, certain pieces become legends not just for their craftsmanship but also for the stories they carry. One such example is the Patek Philippe Calatrava 3594 “Iraqi Eagle”, a timepiece wrapped in intrigue, politics, and exclusivity.

A Unique Relationship Between Power and Prestige

During the mid-1970s and throughout his regime, Saddam Hussein developed an intense appreciation for fine watches—particularly Patek Philippe. His fascination wasn’t just casual collecting; it involved investing millions of dollars into the hands of key individuals within the brand. This financial backing fostered a close, if unofficial, relationship with the Swiss watchmaker.

In gratitude—or perhaps as a form of political tribute—Patek Philippe created a limited series of bespoke Calatrava watches, (he also used other brand like Longines, Universal Geneve, Cartier) personalized with Saddam’s name and the Iraqi coat of arms (the Eagle of Saladin) emblazoned on the dial. These were not pieces for the public, nor were they advertised in catalogs. Instead, they became powerful symbols, gifted to high-ranking officials and loyalists within Saddam’s inner circle.

The Watch: Design and Craftsmanship

The most famous model in this series is the 33 mm Calatrava reference 3594 crafted in 18k white gold, notable for its stepped bezel and snap-back case. What truly sets these watches apart is the stunning blue soleil dial, a rare and elegant touch that perfectly complements the white gold case.

What truly sets these watches apart is the dial: a rare customization featuring the Iraqi eagle alongside Arabic script bearing Saddam Hussein’s name. The design blends the understated elegance of the Calatrava with the weight of political symbolism, creating a watch that’s as much a historical artifact as it is a luxury object.

Remarkably, some of these watches have survived intact with original box and papers, a rarity that adds provenance and desirability for collectors.

Rarity and Historical Significance

The watches were produced in extremely limited numbers—estimates suggest fewer than a dozen personalized pieces exist. This scarcity, combined with the backstory, makes them a coveted but controversial collector’s item.

Owning such a watch means owning a piece of history that is as fascinating as it is fraught. It’s a conversation starter, a collector’s enigma, and a rare example of how personalized craftsmanship can carry a heavy weight far beyond the dial.
